<p>This is a list of arenas that currently serve as the home venue for <b><a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">NCAA</a></b> <b><a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I" title="NCAA Division I">Division I</a></b> <b><a href="/wiki/College_ice_hockey" title="College ice hockey">college ice hockey</a></b> teams. Conference affiliations reflect those in the ongoing <b>2018–19 NCAA Division I men's ice hockey</b> and <b>2018–19 NCAA Division I women's ice hockey</b> seasons.  The arenas serve as home venues for both the men's and women's teams except where noted.  In addition, venues which are not located on campus or are used infrequently during the season have been listed.
</p>

<p>In addition, <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_University" title="Long Island University">Long Island University</a> will add a women's ice hockey program in the 2019–20 school year, but has not yet announced the team's future venue. The team was originally planned to represent <a href="/wiki/LIU_Brooklyn" title="LIU Brooklyn">LIU's Brooklyn campus</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> but shortly after the announcement of a women's hockey program, LIU announced that it would merge the Division I <a href="/wiki/LIU_Brooklyn_Blackbirds" title="LIU Brooklyn Blackbirds">Brooklyn athletic program</a> with the Division II program of its <a href="/wiki/LIU_Post_Pioneers" title="LIU Post Pioneers">Post campus</a>, creating a single D-I athletic program that now competes as the <a href="/wiki/LIU_Sharks" title="LIU Sharks">LIU Sharks</a>. The women's hockey program carried over to the unified athletic program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
